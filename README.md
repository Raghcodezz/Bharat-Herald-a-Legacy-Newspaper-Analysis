# Bharat-Herald-a-Legacy-Newspaper-Analysi

## Business Problem
1) Bharat Herald, a legacy newspaper organization is facing an existential crisis. With a reputation built over seven decades the company once printed over 1.2 million copies daily. 
2) But between 2019 and 2024, print circulation has dropped to under 560,000. The pandemic accelerated a shift toward digital news consumption, and competitors like DigiHindi Post, NewsZilla, and InShorts.
3) Meanwhile, Bharat Herald’s 2021 e-paper pilot failed due to poor mobile usability and was eventually shelved after financial losses. 
4) The result: advertiser confidence declined, vendor payments delayed, and multiple city bureaus shut down which led to losses in the end.

## Goal of the Project
1) As a data analyst we have been assigned to lead a comprehensive analysis of the company’s operational and financial data from 2019–2024. 
2) Quantify what went wrong, 
3) Identify recovery potential
4) Recommend a phased roadmap toward digital transformation.

## Tools used:
1) Python - Data Cleaning
2) SQL - Extracting insights by writing complex queries
3) Excel - Data Manipulation
4) Tableau - Data Visualization
   
## Insights Driven
1) Copies printed, Copies sold, Net circulation of physical newspapers have significantly decreased year on year from 2019-2024.
2) Ad categories like Real Estate and Automobile have shown significant increase in revenue from 2021-2024.
3) Most cities have shown High digital readiness and high digital pilot engagements whereas Kanpur and Ranchi being the outliers where they have good digital readiness score but they have shown least digital pilot engagement scores.
4) Many cities have shown very low increase/no increase in ad revenue over the years but cities like Lucknow, Ranchi and Patna have shown significant increase in ad revenue.
5) Developing cost and Marketing costs for WhatsApp PDF version and Mobile e news paper version are significantly low and people prefer and engage with these two versions when compared to another versions.
6) In 4th quarter of year ( Oct, Nov, Dec ) we see high decline in Net Circulation.
7) No category has revenue 50% more than the total revenue indicating there’s a balance in revenue generated among the categories.
8) Users faced some issues in our digital versions where the font was too small and zooming over it didn’t help, the digital version was not working well in the average phones.  

## Recommendations to the Business
1) As we can see there is a sharp decline copies printed, net circulation, copies sold we can conclude that people are more shifting towards digital content which shift our focus to concentrate on digital version.
2) As Real Estate and Automobile ad categories drive more ad revenue so we should play these type of ads even more to increase the revenue.
3) Kanpur and Ranchi are the cities where the digital readiness score is high but digital pilot score is less thus indicating that we should engage the people in those cities with more interesting content.
4) Lucknow, Patna, Ranchi have shown significant increase in ad revenue because these cities have less number of copies printed thus indicating pushing digital media more can show increase in revenue.
5) WhatsApp PDF and E Mobile paper versions should be the focus region than other versions as they are cheap to produce and have high engagement rate.
6) Phase1 technical bugs should be fixed, Phase2 where focus should be shifted to Mumbai, Delhi, Kanpur, Jaipur by reducing copies printed and promoting digital versions as people are more digitally ready.
7) Collaborate with the social media influencers to promote our digital app and we should also attract journalists with good incentives for their articles.
8) The Digital E Paper and WhatsApp pdf push should be free of cost but people who want to read articles written by journalists then they need to pay a subscription free.
Reward the regular/active users with discount on premium plans.
